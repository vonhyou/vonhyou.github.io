Gladwell once proposed his famous 10,000-Hour Rule in his book, which means that ‘Genius is not a natural talent but a continuous effort, and it takes 10,000 hours at least to go from ordinary to extraordinary.’

As a Chinese student who studies 12 hours a day, six days a week on average, twelve years of dedication has far exceeded the requirements of the 10,000-hour law, and it is reasonable to say that we should all be master learners. But things turn out contrary the way one wishes, the ‘nightmare’ of study efficiency continued to hinder my progress. Fortunately, through the Coursra course “Learn How to Learn”, I realized the importance of scientific study techniques. This article shows how new approaches to learn helped me become more effective.

Similarly, I hope you can learn useful study skills after reading this blog.

Set a Goal, Make a Plan and Gather Infomation#

I decided to spend a few days to verify that the techniques learned in the course were effective in improving my learning. I focused on finding a newer area to reduce the distraction of existing knowledge while not being too rusty for me that it would consume too much time and effort.

After half a day of thinking and preparation, I decided to implement a minimal Lisp Interpreter in 3 days.

Plan First#

During the course, Professor Barbara Oakley repeatedly mentions the importance of planning and recommends making a plan for the next day before going to bed each day to increase efficiency.

So I decided to create an introductory plan to distribute the tasks for the three days in general, and to create a detailed plan based on the tasks to be completed on that day.

My plan is roughly divided into three parts, learning theory, technical implementation, and sharing success.

After meticulous preparation, I started.

Likewise, this article will cover some computer concepts that are very new for most people, and I’m trying to explain them to check if I really understand the knowledge. But I’ll mainly talking about learn techniques instead of computer science or something. If you’re interested in details about this project, you can view its source code on github: lisp-interpreter

A Powerful Tool to Assist Understanding ‚Äî‚Äî Analogy#

Writing an interpreter for a high-level programming language is a highly abstract task in which many concepts are difficult to transfer from real life or other subjects directly.

For example, the lisp use prefix expressions(a.k.a. Polish notation) for arithmatic:

  • normal (a.k.a Infix notation)

$$ 9 + 7 \times (3 - 5) + 2 $$

  • prefix notation

$$ + + 9 \times 7 - 3\,5\,2 $$

and in LISP, it should be

1

(+ (+ 9 (* 7 (- 3 5))) 2)

Letting the computer compute such expressions requires the use of a data structure called stack.

The description of the stack is very abstract and obscure on textbook. It is defined only by the some jargons like ‘operationally constrained linear tables’. Too bad!

But after a few deduction, I realized that this thing called “stack” is really similar to badminton tube in fact.

A stack is a special liner table. Liner means no parallel elements, it’s like not being able to put two balls side by side in a tube. And ‘Limit insert and delete operations only at the end of the table’ can be simply considered that one can only put the ball and take the ball from one end of the tube. (Of course, I assume that you don‚Äôt damage the tube. üëÄ)

So, understanding the concept FILO Ôºà first in last out Ôºâ of stack couldn’t be simpler!

FILO: The element inserted at first in the list, is the last element to be removed from the list

$$ \Downarrow $$

The ball put in at first in the tube is the last to be took out

And proper nouns like push and pop can be easily replaced with put in and take out.

Of course this is only one of many concepts to understand, and I can say that learning by analogy has greatly improved my efficiency and helped me to form chunk

An Excellent Solution for Procrastination ‚Äî‚Äî Pomodoro#

After accumulating a certain amount of knowledge, I started the process of plan-product transformation. This process is more mechanical than learning and sharing, so I need to make sure I’m in constant focus mode. During this time, I turn off the phone’s notification sound, turn on the editor’s Zen mode (One way to isolate notification interference) and put on noise-cancelling headphones ‚Äî‚Äî this process will last for forty minutes.

Why 40 minutes instead of 20 minutes? At first I also tried to take 20 minutes as a cycle, but in practice, I can not write a complete block of code in just 20 minutes, and after interrupting I felt difficult to re-engage, furthermore 40 minutes of coding is a piece of cake for me üòâ

Relax Makes Perfect#

  • Wake up at 5:30, wash up, have an Espresso, study in the morning and breakfast

  • Classes start at 7:20, 45 minutes a class, lunch at 12:00.

  • Nap, class starts at 2:00 pm, until 5:30 pm, dinner

  • Evening study starts at 6:30, one class lasts 55 minutes, back to bed at 10:30 pm

The above is my schedule in high school for three years, very tight. Although college life is a lot easier but inevitably stay up late - sometimes until zero o’clock to sleep. For a long time I treat sleep as a waste of time and tried to avoid it by all means. But now I know that lack of sleep is harmful, and proper physical exercise is also necessary.

So I made some adjustments to my work schedule:

  1. must go to bed at 11:30 every night and get up at 7:15 in the morning
  2. leave one and a half hours for sleep and recreation at noon
  3. jogging 2000 meters every day

In the free time, I fill it with pomodoros and insert break times between them.

The effect of these methods cannot be directly demonstrated, but the completion of the project on schedule is a real success

Reduce Errors Through Communication#

After implementing all the functions, I shared the results of the project on Twitter, partly to share the joy, and partly to hope that the seniors who have some research in this area will give their opinions.

Fortunately, a senior who is studying PLT at The Pennsylvania State University commented on my tweet and pointed out a serious bug. I promptly went and fixed the error. He said afterwards that it took him only a minute to go through my code and find the bug.

That’s when I remembered the point in the course about group learning, which mentioned that effective group work can reveal your mistakes through others.
